Communication Strategy

For most of us, this will be the first time we're all in the same room. We're a fully remote company — and that means there are conversations we've never quite had, context that gets lost in async threads, and connections that haven't had a chance to form yet. This summit is about changing that.

Every message we send should reflect its spirit: warm, intentional, collaborative. Not just logistics — but an invitation to show up fully.

Communication Phases

Phase 1
Build Anticipation
Apr 7 – May 8
Confirm attendance, handle logistics, generate real excitement.
  • "We're bringing the whole team together for the first time."
  • "Everything is taken care of — just focus on getting there."
  • "This week is yours. Hold it."
Phase 2
Set Intentions
May 11 – 29
Help people arrive with curiosity and openness, not just a packed calendar.
  • "We're here to think together, not present at each other."
  • "The conversations that matter most are the ones we haven't had yet."
  • "Come with questions, not just answers."
Phase 3
Live Experience
Jun 1 – 5
Keep energy warm and practical. Reinforce the spirit of the week day by day.
  • Practical daily logistics (lunch, dinners, schedule)
  • Light reflections to carry into each day's sessions
  • Acknowledgment and celebration
Phase 4
Lock It In
Jun 8 – 19
Carry the energy forward. Make sure decisions and connections don't evaporate.
  • What we built together
  • What each person is taking back
  • Reimbursements and next steps

Tone Guidelines

✓ What it should feel like
  • Warm and human — like a colleague you trust
  • Collaborative — shared ownership, not top-down
  • Energizing — a meaningful moment worth looking forward to
  • Clear and practical — helpful, not restrictive
✕ What to avoid
  • Commanding language ("you must", "come ready")
  • Abstract or philosophical framing in logistics
  • Corporate stiffness or policy-heavy tone
  • Anything that feels like a memo instead of a conversation
💡 A good test: would you say this to a colleague over coffee? If yes, it's probably right.

Risk Mitigation

RiskMitigation
People don't read emailsSlack for short nudges + email for official, detailed comms
Confusion about what's coveredOne clear logistics email with a simple coverage table
Low pre-event engagementLight interactive prompts (playlist, one question) to build connection before arrival
Post-summit drop-offFollow-up within 48 hrs of return; ask for one concrete commitment
Reimbursement confusionSend policy early, repeat the key steps post-event

Creative Engagement Ideas

Before the Summit

❓
"One Question" Thread
May 26 — Ask the group: "Summit is one week away — what's one question you're hoping we explore together?" No need to reply to each other. Just reading everyone's questions builds shared anticipation.
🗺️
Attendee Map
Share a simple visual showing where each person is traveling from. Small touch, real impact — it makes the gathering feel meaningful before anyone lands.
🎵
Buenos Aires Playlist
Start a collaborative Spotify playlist in Slack and invite people to add a song. Low effort, high connection — and a fun way to break the ice before arriving.

During the Summit

💬
"One Word" Wall
On Day 1 (Tue), invite everyone to write one word that captures where they are as a leader. On Day 4 (Fri), do it again. The comparison — what shifted, what didn't — tends to spark a great conversation.
📌
Open Questions Board
Set up a physical space (sticky notes, whiteboard) where anyone can post a question or challenge. Others can add their name if they want to think about it together. Creates organic conversations without a formal structure.
📸
Moment of the Day
Ask one person each day to capture one genuine moment — not a posed group photo, but a real one. Collect them into a recap after the summit.
